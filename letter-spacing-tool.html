What is Letter Spacing and Why Does It Matter?

Letter spacing, also known as tracking in professional typography, refers to the uniform adjustment of space between all characters in a block of text. Unlike kerning, which adjusts space between specific character pairs, letter spacing affects every character equally, creating a consistent visual rhythm throughout your text.

Proper letter spacing enhances:

  • Readability: Appropriate spacing makes text easier to read and comprehend
  • Visual Appeal: Well-spaced text looks more professional and polished
  • Brand Identity: Consistent spacing reinforces brand recognition and credibility
  • Emphasis: Strategic spacing can draw attention to important messages
  • Accessibility: Improved spacing helps users with dyslexia and visual impairments
  • Aesthetic Balance: Creates harmony between text and surrounding design elements

What is letter spacing and how is it different from kerning?
Letter spacing (also called tracking) adjusts the space between all characters uniformly throughout your text, while kerning specifically adjusts the space between individual character pairs. Letter spacing affects entire words or text blocks, creating consistent spacing throughout, whereas kerning fine-tunes specific problematic letter combinations like "AV" or "To."
